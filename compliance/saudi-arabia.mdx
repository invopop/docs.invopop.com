## Executive summary

Saudi Arabia mandates electronic invoicing through the **FATOORA** system, operated by the **ZATCA** (Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority). All resident VAT-registered taxpayers must issue invoices and credit/debit notes in a structured XML format from a compliant solution, and — under Phase 2 — must connect that solution to the ZATCA platform. The system splits flows by counterparty: **standard tax invoices** (B2B, B2G) follow a clearance model, where ZATCA must cryptographically stamp the document before it can be shared with the buyer; **simplified tax invoices** (B2C) follow a reporting model, where the document is issued at the point of sale and reported to ZATCA within 24 hours. The system was introduced in phases starting December 2021, with Phase 2 rolling out to taxpayers in waves by VAT-taxable revenue threshold.

## The ZATCA FATOORA system

ZATCA is the tax and customs authority in Saudi Arabia. FATOORA is the e-invoicing platform that VAT-registered taxpayers use to clear standard tax invoices in real time and to report simplified tax invoices within 24 hours of issuance. The invoice is an XML document built on the UBL 2.1 standard with ZATCA-specific extensions, cryptographically stamped by the issuer's solution (an E-invoice Generation Solution, or EGS) using an X.509 certificate issued by ZATCA during onboarding (the CSID — Cryptographic Stamp Identifier).

The scope covers all **resident VAT-registered taxable persons** in the Kingdom, plus any third party issuing invoices on their behalf. Non-resident taxable persons are out of scope.

### Document types

| Type | Counterparty | Flow | Invopop coverage |
|---|---|---|:---:|
| Standard tax invoice | B2B, B2G | Clearance (real-time stamp by ZATCA before sharing with buyer) | Q2 2026 |
| Simplified tax invoice | B2C | Reporting (issued at point of sale, reported to ZATCA within 24h) | Q2 2026 |
| Credit note | Any | Same flow as the invoice it corrects | Q2 2026 |
| Debit note | Any | Same flow as the invoice it corrects | Q2 2026 |

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Standard tax invoice (B2B, B2G)">
    Required for transactions where the buyer is a VAT-registered business or a government entity. Carries the full set of mandatory fields, including the buyer's name, address, and VAT registration number.

    Standard invoices follow the **clearance model**: the issuer's solution submits the XML to ZATCA in real time; ZATCA validates, applies its own cryptographic stamp, and returns the cleared invoice. Only the cleared document is legally valid and shareable with the buyer.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Simplified tax invoice (B2C)">
    Used for transactions with end consumers. The buyer's VAT details are not required and the field set is reduced.

    Simplified invoices follow the **reporting model**: the issuer's solution signs and delivers the invoice to the buyer immediately at the point of sale, then **reports** it to ZATCA within 24 hours. The buyer receives the invoice without waiting for ZATCA to acknowledge it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Credit and debit notes">
    Issued invoices cannot be cancelled or edited after issuance. Corrections are made through:

    - **Credit notes** — reduce the value of a previously issued invoice (returns, post-sale discounts, corrections).
    - **Debit notes** — increase the value of a previously issued invoice.

    Notes must reference the original invoice's number, date and the reason for correction. They flow through the same clearance or reporting channel as the document type they correct.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Implementation phases

ZATCA introduced e-invoicing in two phases.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Phase 1 — Generation (from 4 December 2021)">
    Mandatory for all VAT-registered residents since 4 December 2021. Required taxpayers to:

    - Issue invoices and notes in a structured electronic format from a compliant solution.
    - Stop issuing handwritten or manually edited invoices.
    - Include a QR code on simplified tax invoices.

    Phase 1 did not require integration with ZATCA's platform — the obligation was to generate compliant electronic documents and store them.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Phase 2 — Integration (from 1 January 2023)">
    The integration phase started on 1 January 2023 and is rolling out to taxpayers in **waves** based on their VAT-taxable revenue. In addition to Phase 1 requirements, Phase 2 introduces:

    - Integration of the issuer's solution with the FATOORA platform via API.
    - Cryptographic stamping with a ZATCA-issued certificate (CSID).
    - Real-time clearance of standard invoices and 24-hour reporting of simplified invoices.
    - An expanded QR code carrying the cryptographic stamp and invoice hash.
    - UBL 2.1 XML in line with ZATCA's *Electronic Invoice XML Implementation Standard*.

    Onboarding has progressed through waves at descending revenue thresholds. The most recent announcements:

    | Wave | VAT-taxable revenue threshold | Reference year(s) | Integration deadline |
    |---|---|---|---|
    | 23 | > SAR 750,000 | 2022, 2023 or 2024 | 31 March 2026 |
    | 24 | > SAR 375,000 | 2022, 2023 or 2024 | 30 June 2026 |

    Wave 24 effectively brings every VAT-registered business under the mandatory VAT registration threshold (SAR 375,000) into scope. ZATCA notifies taxpayers in each wave at least six months before their go-live date.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Technical building blocks

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Format and structure">
    Invoices, credit notes and debit notes are **UBL 2.1 XML** documents that follow ZATCA's *Electronic Invoice XML Implementation Standard*, with KSA-specific extensions. Simplified invoices may additionally be presented to the buyer as a PDF/A-3 with the XML embedded.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="QR code">
    Every document carries a Base64-encoded **TLV** (tag-length-value) QR code.

    - **Phase 1**: five tags — seller name, seller VAT registration number, timestamp, invoice total (VAT inclusive), VAT total.
    - **Phase 2**: nine tags — the original five plus the invoice hash, the cryptographic stamp, the public key, and (for standard invoices) ZATCA's signature.

    The QR is mandatory on simplified invoices in Phase 1 and on all documents from Phase 2.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="UUID, ICV and PIH">
    Each document carries three identifiers that secure the integrity of the invoicing chain:

    - **UUID** — a 128-bit unique identifier generated by the issuer's solution for each document.
    - **ICV (Invoice Counter Value)** — a monotonically increasing counter per solution. Values cannot be reused or skipped.
    - **PIH (Previous Invoice Hash)** — the hash of the immediately previous document issued by the same solution, forming a tamper-evident chain.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Cryptographic stamping (CSID)">
    During Phase 2 onboarding, the taxpayer's E-invoice Generation Solution (EGS) is provisioned with an X.509 certificate from ZATCA — the **CSID (Cryptographic Stamp Identifier)**. The solution uses the CSID to:

    - Apply an **ECDSA digital signature** over the document hash (the cryptographic stamp), ensuring integrity and non-repudiation.
    - Authenticate to ZATCA's clearance and reporting APIs.

    For standard invoices, ZATCA adds its own clearance stamp on top of the issuer's stamp before returning the cleared document. For simplified invoices, the issuer's stamp alone is sufficient at point of sale.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Compliance

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Legally required invoice content">
    From ZATCA's *Detailed Guidelines for E-Invoicing* and the *E-Invoicing Implementation Resolution*:

    **Standard tax invoice (B2B, B2G)**

    - Seller name, address and VAT registration number.
    - Buyer name, address and (where the buyer is VAT-registered) VAT registration number.
    - Invoice issue date and supply date if different.
    - Sequential invoice number and UUID.
    - Line-item description, quantity, unit price and any discounts.
    - VAT rate and VAT amount per line.
    - VAT-exclusive subtotal, VAT total and VAT-inclusive total, expressed in SAR.
    - QR code with the nine Phase 2 TLV tags.
    - Invoice Counter Value (ICV) and Previous Invoice Hash (PIH).
    - Cryptographic stamp from the issuer's CSID, plus ZATCA's clearance stamp.

    **Simplified tax invoice (B2C)**

    The simplified invoice carries a reduced field set: seller details, issue date, line items, VAT breakdown, totals in SAR, QR code, UUID, ICV, PIH and the issuer's cryptographic stamp. Buyer details are not required, except in specific cases set by the VAT Implementing Regulation.

    Source: [ZATCA E-Invoicing](https://zatca.gov.sa/en/E-Invoicing/Pages/default.aspx).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Currency">
    Invoices are issued in **Saudi Riyal (SAR)**. Foreign-currency invoices are permitted, but the VAT amount must also be shown in SAR, converted using the SAMA (Saudi Central Bank) exchange rate on the supply date.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="VAT rates">
    Saudi Arabia's standard VAT rate is **15%**, in force since 1 July 2020 (raised from the original 5%).

    - **Standard rate**: 15% — applies to most goods and services.
    - **Zero-rated**: exports outside the GCC, qualifying international transport, qualifying medicines and medical equipment, and investment-grade gold, silver and platinum (≥99% purity).
    - **Exempt**: certain margin-based financial services and residential real-estate rentals.

    Source: [ZATCA VAT rules and regulations](https://zatca.gov.sa/en/RulesRegulations/VAT/Pages/default.aspx).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Cancellations and corrections">
    Issued invoices cannot be cancelled or edited once they have been cleared (standard) or reported (simplified). Any correction must be made by issuing a **credit note** (reduction) or **debit note** (increase) that references the original invoice's number, date and reason for correction.

    Notes follow the same clearance or reporting flow as the document type they correct.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Archival period">
    The VAT Implementing Regulation requires invoices and supporting records to be retained for **at least 6 years** from the end of the relevant tax period. Records must be **stored within Saudi Arabia**; digital archives must reside on servers located in the Kingdom, and originals must not be deleted or altered.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who must comply">
    The obligation applies to all **resident taxable persons** registered for VAT in Saudi Arabia, including any third party issuing invoices on their behalf (e.g. billing service providers).

    Non-resident taxable persons are out of scope of the e-invoicing mandate, even where they are otherwise registered for VAT.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
