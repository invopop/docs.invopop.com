## Executive summary

Poland is shifting to a centralized, clearance-based e-invoicing system called KSeF, routing B2B invoices through one digital hub. Sellers can issue structured XML invoices through an onlinr portal, a dedicated API, or an official mobile app. Before the buyer ever sees it, each invoice is validated in KSeF by the tax authority; once cleared, it gets a unique KSeF ID and is legally issued. The aim is to reduce VAT fraud, increase transparency, and digitize the process of e-invoicing. The Polish tax authority have developed practical touches like QR codes and a 24-hour ‚Äúoffline‚Äù mode to improve usability.

- Every Polish business is required to receive e-invoices from 1 February 2026
- The start date for issuing e-invoices in phased based on company size.
- B2C transactions are out of scope
- Cross-border transactions (B2Bi) e-invoices are generated through KSeF.
- B2G e-invoicing continues through the PEF platform, which is now linked to KSeF.
- Penalties for non compliance are suspended until 31 December 2026

### E-invoicing in Poland

From 2026, domestic B2B invoices will be issued in a strucutred electronic format using the KSeF platform. For ad-hoc or lower volumes, businesses can use the web portal to enter invoices manually. In practice, most organisations access the KSeF platform in their accounting or ERP software using the dedicated API.

Businesses grant the required authorizations (with a KSeF token or certificate), and the system validates, transmits, and receives each e-invoice, ready to view in a company‚Äôs accounting system.

B2G transactions have used e-invoices since 2019, and will not be affected by the new mandate.

### International transactions (B2Bi)

Poland does not introduce a separate e-reporting flow alongside e-invoicing. Domestic B2C remains outside KSeF and continues through online fiscal cash registers sending data to the CRK repository. Cross-border B2B must be issued via KSeF (data reported to tax) and delivered to the foreign buyer outside KSeF (visualization/PDF with KSeF references).

### Regulation

The Ministry of Finance provides free tools to issue and receive structured invoices such as the Taxpayer Application, Mobile application and the e-Mikrofirma app. It publishes implementation manuals and the FA(3) information sheet, which explains formats, field semantics and integration in English.

    A foreign entity is not obliged to issue invoices via KSeF if it has neither a registered office nor a fixed establishment in Poland, or if it has a fixed establishment in Poland that does not participate in the specific supply.

  <Accordion title="Operational notes: QR codes, offline mode, and archiving.">
    - **QR codes:** KSeF provides QR codes to verify whether a document exists in the system and, for offline issuance, to authenticate the issuer. Offline invoices show two QR codes (‚ÄúOFFLINE‚Äù and a certificate QR) until they are delivered to KSeF.
    - **Offline procedures:** In addition to real-time online clearance, KSeF supports: (1) offline24 (to issue now, upload by end of the next business day), (2) scheduled maintenance offline (to upload the day after maintenance ends), and (3) emergency mode (to upload within seven business days after an official outage).
    - **Retention:** Invoices accepted by KSeF are available in the system for 10 years; businesses do not need to operate a separate archive for originals.

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I add attachments to an e-invoice?">

  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if KSeF is temporarily unavailable or I'm offline?">
    Use one of the defined offline modes. For ‚Äúoffline24,‚Äù issue the invoice with the special QR codes and transmit it to KSeF by the end of the next business day.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Will B2C transactions need to be reported in KSeF?">
    No. B2C invoices in KSeF are optional. Receipts via cash registers continue, and receipts with buyer NIP up to PLN 450 remain recognized as simplified invoices through to 31 December 2026.
  </Accordion>
